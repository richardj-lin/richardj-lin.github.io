Walmart

An Accessible Experience

Background

This project was conducted as a part of the capstone project of the Accenture Learning 2 Lead internship.

We chose an Accenture diamond client, and proposed a solution to a problem we noticed they were facing.

The Problem

18%

increase in technology ownership among elderly individuals in the last 4 years.

57%

of elderly individuals over the age of 65 reported finding online shopping sites difficult to navigate.

Since elderly individuals over the age of 65 have the highest discretionary income available out of any generation, and are an underrepresented market of people who could potentially serve as a large customer base for Walmart, then accessibility accommodations through their online shopping sites should be made.

Why Should Walmart Care?

- Walmart generates $441 billion dollars annually from their in-person stores.
- 20% of these shoppers are above the age of 65.
- More than 1 in 5 Americans older than the age of 65 are not driving, meaning they aren’t able to get to these in-person stores by themselves.
- With Walmart’s current accessibility layout, some of these potential customers may not be able to take advantage of Walmart.com.
- This means that Walmart is missing out on upwards of $22 billion dollars annually from this demographic alone, given that Walmart’s annual online sales continue to grow each year.

User Personas

Based on Walmart's Elderly Shopper Demographics

Ivan Kantsei

50 years old

Ivan is a warehouse worker but has difficulty seeing due to his mid-stage cataracts.

Needs

- Prefers not to drive for safety reasons
- Hard to see tiny descriptions of products

Behaviors

- Has difficulty seeing tiny text
- Closely grouped content blurs together for him, making it hard for him to click efficiently

Arthur Ido

75 years old

Arthur is an old man who lives in Greenwood, Mississippi. He has two children, but they live across the United States in San Jose, California. He loves reading physical books.

Needs

- Limit movement due to arthritis
- Reduce stress on finger when clicking

Behaviors

- Cannot stand online books text

Jennifer Park

68 years old

Jennifer Park lives in rural Montana and has Parkinson’s disease. She lives in a retirement home with many of her elderly friends.

Needs

- To be able to shop efficiently and click on the buttons/links
- Needs bigger buttons

Behaviors

- Finds online shopping overwhelming and confusing
- Has trouble clicking small links and buttons on websites and frequently mis-clicks

The solution: easy.access

Easy.access is an accessibility integration to help improve shopping experiences for elderly users and those with physical disabilities or impairments. It will also be compliant with accessibility guidelines such as WCAG 2.2, the ADA, Section 508, and AODA.

Features:
- High contrast mode
- Color/vibrancy controls
- Font/text size controls
- Voice commands
- Cursor size
- Ability to purchase items through phone without needing to click or scroll often
- Easy Access Mode, a completely revamped interface designed for simple user and navigation

General Design Goals: Larger text, simpler navigation, larger content, cleaner (less cluttered) feel.
